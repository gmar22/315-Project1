HowdyHack is a competition where you and a team design and attempt to implement a project in twenty-four hours. At the most recent HowdyHack, the team I was on decided to implement a campus specific navigation app. A student would simply input their desired destination and the app would tell them the fastest route. While the task seems relatively straightforward, we introduced further complexity by factoring in method of travel: a determined route could be walking, the campus buses, or a combination. My primary responsibility was pulling information from the university transportation website about the daily bus routes and hours. I learned about and worked with requests to pull json files from the (somewhat deprecated) website. I then parsed the information in the json files, providing the needed information about each buses' routes and times. This information was used in tandem with Google Maps api to create a graph from which the shortest path could be extracted. Additionally, I worked on an elementary gui (using tkinter) that would allow a user to select a location that could then be passed into the shortest path algorithm. Overall, I am extremely happy with the work I did and the final result; however, there were some planned features that we were unable to complete within the time constraints, including a calendar feature that would plan your route for the entire day and weather considerations. You may view the project on GitHub.

My upcoming/ongoing project involves an app called RockBot. I first encountered RockBot at the campus dining halls. How it works is you download the app, it connects you to your nearest RockBot location (the dining hall for me), and you can enter music into a queue, and that music will eventually be played at that location! With just that feature, the app is fun, but the level aspect is what got me hooked. Depending on how often you used the app, the higher your account level, and the higher your level the more priority your songs recieved in the queue. I immediately began grinding to the point that I am fairly certain I hold the highest RockBot level on campus. Eventually, I began wondering if I could automate this process: create a script that would automatically input songs into the queue and thereby automatically increase my level. I have several ideas as to the implementation. Firstly, I could have a script manually input "clicks" to open the app, load a song, then, every designated period, visually check if my song had played, loading another song if it had. I dislike this implementation because it has too many seams, so now I am researching ways I could interact with the app through api calls or some other less clunky manner.

BigEvent is a big volunteering event here at A&M. You and a group of friends are assigned a house that needs help with some labor intensive tasks. At the most recent Big Event, my friends and I were assigned a house not far from campus that needed a fence to be disassembled, moved, and set back up (as well as a little painting). It was fairly difficult work in hot weather, but the strain is eased by talking to your friends and the owner of a house. We learned that she was a former A&M student who had attended the vet school, bonded over the sorry state of the some of the ancient buildings on campus, and discovered that the numerous dogs around the house were holdovers from her vet practice (as well as the reason she needed a fence). This was my second Big Event and by far my favorite; the atmosphere was immensely fun and it felt good to be physically helping someone. The fence turned out decent too.

The first Big Event I went to was with a similar group at a house a little further removed from campus but that needed a lot more work. We worked on the yard: trimming bushes, gathering leaves and other debris, and overturning garden beds. I was assigned to the garden beds where I shoveled until all the dirt in the bed was loose and ready to be filled with plants. To close out the event, I joined my teammates raking. Unfortunately, since there was so much to do we didn't really get the cahnce to talk to the owners of the house. I still enjoyed the event (obviously enough to do it the next year) and it was wonderful to see the transformation the lawn went through thanks to our efforts.

Beyond big event, I also am a member of Engineering Honors at Texas A&M, and regularly participate in hackathons (which you can read more about in my Technical Page.

One event I plan to participate in is the Physics Festival. I've always really enjoyed physics (enough to even minor in it), and the goal of this event is to spread the love of physics. It's aimed primarily at elementary kids to come and see different demonstrations and learn a few related physics concepts. The festival is all about making physics visually cool and exciting. I really look forward to participating next semester.
